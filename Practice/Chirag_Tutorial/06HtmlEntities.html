HTML Entities

If you use the less than < (<) > or greater than < (>) > signs in your text, the browser might mix them with tags. Character entities are used to display reserved characters in HTML.A character entity looks like this:

A commonly used entity in HTML is the non-breaking space: (nbsp)   A non-breaking space is a space that will not break into a new line.Two words separated by a non-breaking space will stick together (not break into a new line). This is handy when breaking the words might be disruptive.

&  ampersand  & nbsp 
"  double quotation mark  & quot
' single quotation mark (apostrophe) & apos
¢ cent & cent
£ pound & pound
¥ yen & yen
